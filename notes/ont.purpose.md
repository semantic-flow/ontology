## Why This Ontology Exists

The ontology exists to support Semantic Flow identifiers and their
publication as Semantic Web IRIs that are human-dereferenceable and
machine-usable in a predictable way. It formalizes how a Semantic Flow
identifier, its associated `Knop`, and the `Knop`'s reference, metadata,
inventory, and payload resources fit together, so tools can consistently
discover what an identifier is about and how its supporting Semantic Flow
structures are organized.

It also exists to formalize the core structure of digital artifacts: their
continuity over time, their historical states, their manifestations, and
the located files that provide retrievable bytes. That structure makes it
possible to model publishing and editing workflows cleanly, including
immutable access to historical versions, without tying the model to any one
serialization strategy or requiring every nearby file to be part of a
mesh.
